# A weekly YouTube digest that helps my wife choose what to watch

## Alternative short titles

- From podcast backlog to weekly shortlist
- A practical AI digest for YouTube podcasts
- Helping one person choose better, faster

## Headline

I built a weekly AI-assisted YouTube digest so my wife can quickly decide which podcast episodes are worth watching.

## Problem

My wife follows several favourite YouTube and podcast-style channels. The problem was not a lack of content — it was too much content.

Every week there were new long-form videos, overlapping topics, and no easy way to know which ones were most relevant without opening each one.

## Why it mattered

This was a small household problem, but it looked a lot like a business problem:

- too many inputs;
- limited attention;
- inconsistent metadata;
- useful information buried inside long content; and
- a person needing a clear, low-effort decision aid.

That made it a good practical AI use case: not flashy, but genuinely useful.

## Solution

I created a weekly YouTube digest script that checks around ten favourite channels, finds newly published videos, exports available transcripts, and summarises each video into three key bullet points.

The final output is a clean email with screenshots or thumbnails, video links, and short summaries written for quick scanning.

The aim is simple: help her decide what to watch, not replace the original creators or pretend the summary is the full experience.

## Outcome

The digest turns a scattered weekly backlog into one readable email.

Instead of opening every new video, she can scan the shortlist, see the main ideas, and choose the episodes that look most useful or interesting.

It is a practical example of AI helping with a real decision in everyday life.

## What it proves

This project shows that I can:

- spot a real problem in daily life and turn it into a workflow;
- connect content discovery, transcript handling, AI summarisation, and email delivery;
- design outputs for a real human reader;
- keep the language simple and useful;
- handle privacy and copyright boundaries carefully; and
- use AI as a practical assistant, not a magic trick.

## Privacy note

This public version uses demo data only. It does not publish real transcripts, private API tokens, real recipient details, or any personal channel preferences that should stay private.

Any sample transcript content should be fictional or otherwise safe to share. The case study is about the workflow and the problem-solving approach, not about redistributing creator content.
